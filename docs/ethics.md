# Ethics and Responsible Research

This document describes the ethical principles and constraints that guide BDM as a research project. These are not aspirational statements — they are binding guidelines that shape what the project claims, how it communicates, what it builds, and what it refuses to do.

---

## No Medical Claims

BDM is not a medical product, medical research tool, clinical instrument, or therapeutic application.

BDM does not claim to:
- diagnose, treat, prevent, or cure any medical condition
- improve cognitive function in any measurable clinical sense
- support decision-making in healthcare, psychology, or neurology
- be safe or appropriate for use in clinical or therapeutic contexts

Any person seeking cognitive health support, neurological evaluation, or psychological assistance should consult a qualified medical or clinical professional.

If BDM is ever used in any research context that approaches human subjects or health-related data, that work must follow applicable ethical review processes, institutional guidelines, and legal requirements. This document does not constitute or substitute for an ethics review.

---

## No Consciousness Claims

BDM does not claim to:
- create consciousness in a software system
- simulate consciousness or approximate subjective experience
- prove that a system is or is not conscious
- produce systems that "feel," "experience," or have inner states in any meaningful sense

The use of terms like "mind," "deep mind," "self-model," and "reflection" in this project is metaphorical and functional. These terms describe software design patterns, not properties of awareness, sentience, or subjective experience.

The question of whether a system is conscious is not resolved by the presence of a self-model or memory layer. BDM takes no position on the conditions for consciousness and makes no claims in that direction.

---

## No Brain Upload or Mind Transfer Claims

BDM does not claim to:
- copy a human mind into software
- upload, transfer, preserve, or extend a human consciousness
- provide any path toward digital immortality or mind continuity
- replicate the structure or function of a human brain

BDM is software research. It draws conceptual inspiration from cognitive science but does not operate on biological data and does not model actual brain processes. The distinction between "inspired by cognitive concepts" and "replicating the brain" is categorical, and BDM is firmly in the former category.

---

## Responsible Language

BDM commits to using careful, precise language in all documentation, code comments, publications, and communications.

This means:
- Framing all unverified ideas as hypotheses, not facts
- Distinguishing clearly between a model, a simulation, and actual cognition
- Not using language that implies human-like awareness or experience in a software system
- Not using language designed to generate excitement at the cost of accuracy
- Acknowledging limitations explicitly and prominently
- Updating or retracting claims when evidence does not support them

Language to avoid: "the system thinks," "the system feels," "the system is aware," "digital consciousness," "brain in software," "copy of a mind," "artificial soul."

Language to prefer: "the system stores," "the system retrieves," "the system models," "the system represents," "a structured approximation," "a conceptual layer," "a research hypothesis."

---

## Privacy Concerns

BDM's memory and continuity layers will store records of interactions, including potentially sensitive content. This raises privacy considerations that the project takes seriously:

- Memory stores should not collect or retain personally identifiable information without explicit consent
- Stored interaction records should be protected from unauthorized access
- Users should have the ability to inspect, export, and delete stored records
- Any experiment involving user data must operate under a clear data handling policy
- BDM will not share stored interaction data with third parties without explicit consent

As the project progresses from conceptual phase to prototype, specific data handling protocols will be documented and published.

---

## Safety Concerns

BDM is an early-stage research project. Safety considerations include:

- **Behavioral drift:** A learning loop that updates stored beliefs without adequate safeguards could reinforce incorrect or harmful beliefs over time. All learning mechanisms must include logging, auditability, and revert capability.
- **Over-reliance:** Users must not be encouraged to rely on BDM's outputs for consequential decisions, particularly in domains like health, legal, or financial matters.
- **Reflection failures:** A reflection module that incorrectly flags consistent outputs as inconsistent, or fails to flag genuine inconsistencies, could degrade output quality in non-obvious ways. All reflection components must be evaluated carefully before deployment.
- **Model limitations:** BDM's reliance on LLMs as a substrate means it inherits all known limitations of those models, including hallucination, bias, and context-window constraints.

---

## Human-Centered Research

BDM is designed for and by humans, with human needs and human oversight in mind.

This means:
- The project does not pursue autonomy at the expense of transparency
- Users should understand what the system stores, retrieves, and reasons about
- No component of BDM should operate in a way that is deliberately opaque to its users or developers
- The goal is to build useful, honest, auditable software — not to maximize capability without regard for consequences

---

## Transparency and Limitations

BDM commits to transparency about what it is and what it is not:

- All published results will include a clear statement of limitations
- Negative results will be published alongside positive ones
- The project will not cherry-pick findings to present a more favorable picture
- Architectural descriptions will clearly distinguish between implemented components and conceptual ones
- Version history and change logs will be maintained openly

---

## Avoiding Deceptive Anthropomorphism

Anthropomorphism — attributing human characteristics to non-human systems — is a known risk in AI research and communication. BDM takes care to avoid deceptive anthropomorphism:

- Software layers will not be described as if they have experiences, intentions, or feelings
- User interfaces will not be designed to create a false impression of sentience or emotional responsiveness
- Documentation will not use language that encourages users to form inappropriate attachments or trust levels with the system

Functional metaphors (e.g., "the system reflects on its prior output") are acceptable when clearly framed as descriptions of computational processes, not as claims about inner experience.

---

## Simulation, Modeling, and Actual Cognition

These three concepts are categorically distinct, and BDM will maintain that distinction at all times:

- **Simulation** — a software process that mimics certain observable behaviors of a system, without necessarily implementing the same mechanisms
- **Modeling** — a structured representation of selected properties of a system, abstracted from its full complexity
- **Actual cognition** — the processes occurring in biological brains, involving mechanisms that are not yet fully understood

BDM builds models. It may produce simulations of certain cognitive behaviors. It does not replicate, access, or reproduce actual cognition.
